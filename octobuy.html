OctoBuy

UI/UX Design

Project Brief:

My task was to develop a user-friendly service that combines the functions of buying various items, selling those items safely for the people who simply want to get rid of the extra stuff they have at home, as well for the professionals who want to extend the area of their businesses. The challenge was to provide a safe environment for both buyer and seller minimising the possibility of cheating or scamming.

Phase 1: Research

In order to better understand the needs and priorities of potential OctoBuy customers, my partner and I conducted a competitive analysis of similar applications to identify common features and content of their websites.

The primary and secondary research generated critical insights into the expectations people have when they want to buy or sell something. The Buyers wanted to have a smooth experience, have the confidence that they won’t be scammed. And the Sellers wanted to be sure they can be protected as well. The process also helped us clarify the essential information and interactions that we would need to offer on the OctoBuy website in order to facilitate desirable and successful engagement with current and future customers.

Additionally, the research was critical for pinpointing target audiences. Based on the research and user interviews, we were able to identify 4 user personas and making 2 primary user personas: Seller and Buyer.

Two primary user personas

Phase 2: Information architecture: user flows and customer journey map

I used the research findings and stakeholder needs to articulate the proposed information architecture through the various user flows for different kinds of persona.

Based on the various user personas and their frustrations, I also thought of the feelings users might experience landing on the website and what I, as a designer, should be able to change in order to improve their experience.

Phase 3: Wireframing + Prototyping

Through diagramming user flows, I identified key pages that I would need to design, and sketched them with pen and paper. I quickly transitioned to designing a low-fidelity prototype in Adobe Xd.

Phase 4: Usability testing + Iteration + User Interface

After wireframing and prototyping an initial set of pages, I conducted usability testing in order to identify potential errors and issues that people might encounter when engaging with the prototype. I tested the website with people who might use such services in the future in order to better understand the level of clarity of the site content and structure, especially to people who are new to, or unfamiliar with such type of websites.

The key findings from Usability Testing

Sellers were lacking the amount of views of the page with the product
The “Upload Photos” button wasn’t clear enough
The initial message icon wasn’t clear/visible enough
The categories that were shown on a search page confused some users

After a few iterations and edits, I managed to come to the final version of the prototype and work on the User Interface.
