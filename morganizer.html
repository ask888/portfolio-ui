Morganizer

UI/UX Design

Project Brief:

It's a very common problem to allocate time correctly and to begin your morning organized. A lot of successful people say that the morning is the most important part of the day to set up yourself to a productive and successful day.

As people nowadays are busy and live a fast-paced life, it's no wonder they could experience issues with starting the day most efficiently. And our user is exactly the same.

User persona:

After defining our user persona and setting up the main goals we moved to a customer journey map. A customer journey map tells us the story of the customer's experience: from initial contact, through the process of engagement and into a long-term relationship.

In our app, the target persona Jane was experiencing frustration while being sometimes disorganized and consequently not having time for her usual preparation for tomorrow. And when she begins to use the Morganizer she realizes how easy it is to be efficient from the very morning and having done a lot of important things before even the working day has started.

Customer Journey Map:

We also created a user flow to show the path taken by our prototypical user on the app to complete a task. The user flow takes our persona from their entry point through a set of steps towards a successful outcome and final action, such as completing the tasks and feeling satisfied.

Furthermore, we developed the first basic paper sketches to see how our users will interact with the app. During the testing process, we had to extend the prototype by adding additional screens to make the exploring process easier.

In some cases, we had to get rid of the features we planned to include into a prototype because some testers weren't using them. For instance, we had a feature of setting up the time for each of the tasks to see how long it will take our user to complete the plan they have made. But in the end, we realized that at this stage our target audience doesn't need to track the time they spend.
