Namaste

This is a redesign of a recreational cannabis website. Both UX and UI parts are done by me. Previous version of the website was created from a template and didn’t have all the features the stakeholders wanted, as well as it was not SEO friendly.

The current website is responsive, user and SEO friendly, and meets all the stakeholder requirements. It also speaks about Namaste brand as well as provides the necessary information about the products for the users.

Moreover, my team and I have improved blog experience, adding more links for users to stay entertained but not overwhelm with them.

The animations of the “Breathe in-Breathe Out” on home page, “Burning incense” on Our Story page were done by me in Adobe After Effects.

The prototype of Namaste was created and tested on users in Adobe Xd.

The case study, screenshots of before and after are available upon request.

Re-Up

This recreational cannabis one page website was created from scratch in collaboration with a graphic designer, who has created a logo and a brand story.

My role was to design a responsive website that would be minimalistic, plain and adhere the style of the logo. It should also be clear for the users what it is about and how Re-Up is different from other brands.

Neigun

This is my freelance project, the redesign of the website that looked like it was created in 90s. The client asked me to create a modern, user-friendly website that would promote the product that he produces himself - a metal whisk that is used in Taoist practices to support and rejuvenate the bones and body system.

Doing my research, I realised that the majority of the visitors on the website are people who are not frequent users of the internet. The challenge was to create a modern, yet straightforward experience with clear designs that would be easy to navigate and get the necessary information.

The Before and After screenshots are available upon request.

Zenabis Interview Information Guide

This project was created with the aim to support the interviewees and make their interview experience with the company as smooth as possible.

This project has a slightly unusual format of an information-carousel that users could go through as well as jump to a particular page using navigation at the top.

The stakeholders also wanted for it to be engaging and unusual in comparison to other brands, answering most of the questions interviewees might have.
